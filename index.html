01 — Who I Am

About Me

Hey there! I'm Dipika Bhelaye — a Computer Engineering student at MIT Academy of Engineering, Pune, currently in my 2nd semester, navigating the beautiful chaos of circuits, code, and creativity.

My story began in Wardha, Maharashtra — at Gandhi City Public School, where curiosity was my constant companion. 02 — Where It Began

The Origin Story

Every journey has a moment — a spark. Mine came in Class 8, when I first stumbled into the world of app development and realised that technology wasn't just something you used — it was something you could build.

I found my way to the Technovation Girls Challenge, a global competition that dares young women to solve real-world problems through mobile apps. Our team made it to the India Semifinals — and suddenly, building tech felt personal, purposeful, and electric.

"The first time I presented a solution I built from scratch to an audience, something shifted in me permanently."

By Class 10, that spark had grown into a flame. I built Empower — an app designed to support survivors and raise awareness around domestic violence. It wasn't just code; it was a statement.

That project was recognised by the India Book of Records — a humbling moment that reminded me why I started. Not for accolades, but because technology, when built with heart, can change lives.

Those formative years taught me to pitch ideas, learn from failure, think like a problem-solver, and always ask — "who does this help?" That question still guides everything I build.
